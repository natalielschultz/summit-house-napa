## Internet & Connectivity

### Starlink Internet

The property uses **Starlink satellite internet**, which provides fast, reliable high-speed service even at this remote mountain elevation. Starlink uses a low-orbit satellite network, so speeds are strong for streaming, video calls, large downloads, and remote work.

- **Network:** Natalie1
- **Password:** 3119MtVeederaframe

### If the Internet Goes Down

1. Check the Starlink router — it's located near the kitchen area
2. Unplug the router power cable, wait 30 seconds, plug it back in
3. Allow 2–3 minutes for it to reconnect
4. If it doesn't come back, check whether the Starlink dish outside has a clear view of the sky (heavy snow or debris can block it)
5. If still down after 10 minutes, contact us

> ℹ️ Internet speed may be diminished or interrupted during power outages. Starlink requires power to operate.

### Cell Service

A-Frame is a rural property, which means cell service may be spotty depending on your carrier. If you're experiencing issues, we recommend enabling **WiFi calling** on your phone — it usually solves the problem.

- iPhone: Settings → Phone → WiFi Calling → turn on
- Android: Settings → Connections → WiFi Calling → turn on

At the house, most carriers get about one bar — enough for calls and texts. However, **there is no cell service on the drive up** to the property, so don't be alarmed if your phone loses signal on the way. It should reconnect once you arrive. We strongly recommend enabling WiFi calling once you're connected to the Starlink network.

## Sauna

There is an **Enlighten Sauna Rustic 4C** outdoor infrared sauna located in the corner of the back garden. It uses far-infrared and near/mid-infrared heating elements for deep, even warmth. The control panel provides access to temperature, timer, lighting, and music.

### How to Use

1. Turn the sauna on and allow **15–20 minutes** to preheat (until it reaches about 110°F)
2. Close the ceiling vent to speed up preheating. Open it during your session if you need fresh air.
3. Place a towel on the bench before sitting
4. Most guests set the temperature to around **125°F**
5. Sessions can run up to 40 minutes. The timer can be set from 5 to 60 minutes.
6. **Pro tip:** Step in about 10 degrees before the sauna reaches your target temperature — the infrared heaters deliver the most benefit while they're actively heating up
7. **Quick start:** Press the PT button on the control panel to automatically set 113°F for 60 minutes

The sauna has a built-in speaker system with **Bluetooth, FM radio, USB, and AUX input**. Press the Bluetooth button on the control panel and pair from your phone.

It also includes **chromotherapy (color therapy) lights** — use the included remote to select a single color or press Cycle to rotate through all colors during your session.

> ℹ️ A detailed **Infrared Sauna Guest Guide** is provided separately with full information on the heating zones, health benefits, color therapy, and safety guidelines. The full manufacturer's manual (Enlighten Sauna Rustic 4C) is also available on request.

### Sauna Rules

> ⚠️ If you are not experienced using a sauna, please use additional caution.

**Medical:**

- Pregnant women and persons with heart disease, diabetes, high/low blood pressure, circulatory or respiratory problems, or seizure conditions should consult a doctor before use
- **Limit sessions to a maximum of 15 minutes** due to fatiguing effects
- Do not use the sauna under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or medications that affect circulation
- **Leave immediately** if you experience nausea, dizziness, hot flashes, cold chills, or headaches
- Drink plenty of fluids before and after

**Safety:**

- Children 5 and under are not permitted in the sauna
- Children 6–16 must be accompanied by an adult at all times
- Do not use the sauna alone, as a medical situation may arise
- The heater surface and surrounding area can be extremely hot — exercise caution
- Remove all metal jewelry before entering
- No smoking, eating, drinking, exercising, or reading materials inside the sauna
- Do not splash water on the heaters or any electrical components inside the sauna
- Sit on a towel to protect the cedar benches

### After Use

Turn off the sauna when you're done. Leave the door slightly cracked for a few minutes to ventilate. Do not leave towels, water bottles, or any items inside the sauna after your session — the interior must stay dry.

- **Deck fire pit** — small propane tank next to the outdoor couch

If additional tanks are needed during a longer stay, they can be delivered at an added charge.

### What You Need to Know

- Propane is managed by us — you do not need to order or refill the main tank
- If you smell gas (rotten egg smell) anywhere in the house, **leave immediately** and call us, then call 911 if needed
- Do not store anything near or on top of the propane tank

### If an Appliance Won't Ignite

If a stove burner or the grill won't ignite, try lighting it manually with a long match or lighter while turning the gas knob. If it still won't light or you smell gas without a flame, turn the knob off, ventilate the area, and contact us.

## Seasonal Living Guide

### Spring (March – May)

Spring is the best weather of the year on Mount Veeder. Temperatures are mild and comfortable, and the hillsides come alive with wildflowers and lush green growth. It's a beautiful time to explore the meditation trail and spend time on the deck. Occasional rain is possible — check the forecast if you're planning outdoor time.

### Summer (June – September)

This is peak outdoor season. The deck, hot tub, sauna, zen garden, and outdoor shower are all at their best. Mount Veeder stays significantly cooler than the valley floor, especially in the evenings — you may want a light layer after sunset even in July.

Be aware that summer is also fire season in Napa County. We'll notify you of any burn bans or restrictions. PG&E occasionally issues Public Safety Power Shutoffs (PSPS) during extreme fire weather — these have happened at the property before. If one is announced, we'll let you know and help you prepare (see Power Outages section).

### Fall (October – November)

Fall is arguably the best time to visit Napa. Harvest season is in full swing in the valley — the energy, the colors, and the cooler evenings make it special. The leaves change on the mountain, and the air gets crisp. It's a great time for the hot tub and fire pit after a day of wine tasting. Evenings start getting cool enough to layer up.

### Winter (December – February)

Winter on Mount Veeder means cold nights, heavy rain at times, and shorter daylight hours. It doesn't snow up here, and the road handles winter conditions fine — no closures or special precautions needed. The hot tub and sauna are at their most rewarding when it's cold outside. Pipes freezing has never been an issue at this property. Layer up for evenings on the deck, and enjoy the quiet — winter on the mountain is peaceful.

## Wildlife & Safety

You're on 2 acres of mountain forest. Wildlife is part of the experience — and something to respect.

### Common Wildlife

- **Deer** — frequent visitors, harmless. They may eat garden plants.
- **Raccoons** — active at night. Keep trash secured and don't leave pet food outside.
- **Coyotes** — you may hear them at night. Keep small pets indoors after dark.
- **Wild turkeys** — common in the area, harmless but bold.

### Less Common but Important

- **Mountain lions** — rarely seen, but present in the area. If you see one, do not run. Make yourself large, make noise, back away slowly. Keep children close on trails.
- **Rattlesnakes** — possible in warmer months (April–October). Watch where you step on trails and rocky areas. Do not reach under rocks or logs.
- **Black bears** — occasional. Secure all food and trash. If you encounter one, make noise and give it space to leave.

### Ticks & Poison Oak

- **Ticks** are present in tall grass and wooded areas. Check yourself after walks. Wear long pants on the meditation trail if you're concerned.
- **Poison oak** — "leaves of three, let it be." Common along trails. Learn to recognize it. If you touch it, wash the area with soap and cold water immediately.

### General Safety

- Carry a flashlight after dark — the mountain gets very dark
- Stay on marked paths and the meditation trail
- Make noise when walking at dusk or dawn (clap, talk, sing) to alert wildlife
